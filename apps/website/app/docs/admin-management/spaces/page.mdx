# Spaces & Rooms

Spaces let you organize your devices by physical location — rooms, floors, and outdoor areas. This makes it easier
to manage devices, build dashboards, and create scenes that target specific areas of your home.

---

## How Spaces Work

Spaces use a two-level hierarchy:

- **Zones** — larger groupings like floors or outdoor areas (e.g., "Ground Floor", "Backyard")
- **Rooms** — individual spaces within zones (e.g., "Living Room", "Kitchen", "Bedroom")

Rooms can optionally belong to a zone, but they can also exist independently.

---

## Zone Categories

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Floors | Ground Floor, First Floor, Second Floor, Basement, Attic |
| Outdoor | Front Yard, Backyard, Garden, Terrace, Balcony, Driveway |
| Utility | Utility areas, Security perimeter |

---

## Room Categories

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Living areas | Living Room, Bedroom, Guest Room, Nursery |
| Kitchen & dining | Kitchen, Dining Room, Pantry |
| Service areas | Bathroom, Toilet, Laundry |
| Utility | Garage, Workshop, Office, Storage, Closet, Utility Room |
| Entertainment | Media Room, Gym |
| Access | Hallway, Entryway |

## Adding a Space

<Steps>
	### Click **+ Add**

	In the **Spaces** section, click the add button to create a new zone or room.

	<div style={{ textAlign: 'center' }}>
		![Add a new space](/images/modules/spaces/02_space_add.png)
	</div>

	### Fill in Details

	- **Name** — display name (e.g., "Living Room")
	- **Type** — choose `Zone` or `Room`
	- **Category** — specific type from the tables above
	- **Parent zone** *(rooms only)* — optionally assign to a zone
	- **Icon** — MDI icon identifier (e.g., `mdi:sofa`)
</Steps>

## Deleting a Space

When you delete a zone, its child rooms become orphaned — they still exist but lose their parent.
When you delete a room, its assigned devices are unassigned.
